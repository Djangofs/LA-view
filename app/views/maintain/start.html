Overview

All food businesses which regularly handle, produce or serve food to the public need to be registered with their Local Authority by law. They are also required to notify their local council of any significant change to the business, including closing.

During this registration, you may come across a few specialist terms, which we have described below:

Food business operator

The operator is the person, charity or company who makes the decisions about the food business, what it serves and how it operates.

Establishment

The establishment is the location or site of your food business. If it is a mobile food business, please use the location where it is normally stored overnight.

Register a new food business

You are only required to register a new food business 28 days before they begin trading. If you are already trading and have not registered you need to do so as soon as possible. If you operate more than one premises, you need to register each of them with the local authority in which they are located.

Update a food business

You must update a food business with any significant changes within 28 days of them happening.

Close a food business

You must close a food business within28 days of it happening.
